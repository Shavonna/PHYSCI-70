3d Printing, Prusa, and more.

For this week,I learned how to use the 3d printers, and the Prusa slicer software. Because I wanted to get familiarity with the printers as well as prototype designs for my final project, I decided to focus on earrings that I might include in my final project.


Object 1: Tree Earrings (Geometric)

  • For these first pair of earrings, I wanted to make sure prusa would be able to print earrings at a reasonable size well. This turned out fairly successful! However, since my print was overnight, I came back to retrieve my tree earrings, and someone had moved them off the plate. Because it does not look like the printer failed, and likely someone was impatient and broke my earrings as they removed them from the plate, I consider this first print a success. I am still thinking on how I would attach speakers to these without drastically increasing the weight to an unbearable amount. However, I am still glad that I was able to make these and they printed out very nicely. In the future, I would hope to make more thematic designs and maybe even make speaker shaped lightweight and hollow earrings to fit whatever electronic components are needed.

    • Object 2: Bumble bee earring, Fusion Design

  • For the second pair of earrings, I desinged in fusion a bumble bee like earring which hangs upside down, and then translated those designs to prusa. This was another great learning exercise in both manipulating sketches on fusion to make wearable 3d printable art, and also thinking about what items/objects are related to sound (like the buzz of a bee) and would make sense in my project of wearable fashion tech. I also had to remove the supports for this object, which was honestly really scary! Figuring out how to get those suprisingly sturfy supports removed without damaging the bofy of the earring itself was a bit difficult. but luckily I figured it out with the help of other members in the lab.

    • Object Printability Bumble bee earring and Tree Earrings

  • When thinking about 3d printing these designs, I wanted to make sure it would be something that could not be easily made via the laser printer or the handsaws/etc. This would mean that the objects would have to be complex in shape (not be 2d or simply 2d objects built upon each other), and would also need to have intricate 3d details, (like the geometric pattern in the tree earrings or the eyes and wings of the bumble bee ones) that would make them nearly impossible to make any other way. In fact, although you coulf make similar but simpler designs with the laser cutter, I wholeheartedly believe that both of these items could only truly be made with the 3d printers.
